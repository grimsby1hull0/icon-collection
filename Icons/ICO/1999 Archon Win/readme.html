From the Artist

December 1999

Archon by Dave Brasgalla

It’s hard to miss the fact that “retro” video gaming is in! Amid the sound and fury of today’s 3D powerhouse games, there’s a part of us that really hankers for the sprite-based, pixelly goodness of home computing’s formative period. Steven Frank of Panic software contacted me recently about helping resurrect one of his old Apple faves, “Archon”. While the actual game update is still largely theoretical, the icons are now in your hands!

These icons are dedicated to Jim Nitchals, who died in 1998.

Best known for his work on the Apple title that was the first title to actually work background music into the game loop, a feat that was never again accomplished without the use of additional hardware.

Following his early titles, Nitchals moved on to EA where he worked on numerous projects. According to Nitchals own web page, one of his most memorable projects was his port of Archon to the Apple ][ and Mac.

One last note: I want to thank Bob Nystom at Robot Frog for graciously allowing me to totally cop his style. If you like these icons, please check out Bob’s work – I am just a pale imitation of The Master!

Also – as always – special thanks to Kate… See You Next Thursday!

Enjoy! Dave
